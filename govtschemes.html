Government Schemes for Women

The Government of India has introduced several schemes aimed at improving the lives of women through financial assistance, education, employment, and healthcare support.

Major Schemes

  • Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP): Focuses on saving and educating the girl child.
  • Janani Suraksha Yojana (JSY): Provides safe motherhood interventions.
  • Mahila Shakti Kendra: Empowers rural women through skill development.
  • Working Women Hostel Scheme: Safe accommodation for working women.
  • Ujjawala Scheme: Prevents trafficking and provides rehabilitation support.

Impact

These initiatives aim to provide women with education, security, healthcare, and employment opportunities, ensuring equality and social development.

Here are some Maharashtra-specific schemes and initiatives for women & girls, with details on what they provide, eligibility etc.: Major Maharashtra Schemes for Women / Girls 1:Mukhyamantri Majhi Ladki Bahin Yojana (Ladki Bahin / Majhi Ladki Bahin) =Monthly financial assistance of ₹1,500 to eligible women aged 21-65 years. Women who are permanent residents of Maharashtra; non-taxpayers generally; excludes government employees etc. 2:Lek Ladki Yojana Provides financial assistance of ~₹1,01,000 in total, from birth up to age 18, for girls’ health, education and development. For girls born in Maharashtra, under specified conditions. It aims to reduce child mortality, encourage school attendance, prevent child marriage etc. 3:Majhi Kanya Bhagyashree Scheme On birth of girl child, fixed deposit given: ₹50,000 for one girl; if there are two girls, each child gets ₹25,000. Also includes accident insurance, facility to withdraw interest periodically. For girl children born on or after 1st August 2017; family income criteria applies (both BPL and APL families) and other eligibility norms. 4:Mission Shakti (Maharashtra) A mission-mode scheme focusing on women’s safety, security, and empowerment, with lifecycle approach; includes convergence of departments, local governance (Panchayats), digital infrastructure, etc. Covers a wide range of women’s issues; many sub-programs like “Women Corner” etc under its umbrella. 5:Punyashlok Ahilya Devi Holkar Mahila Startup Yojana A scheme to support women entrepreneurs via financial assistance ranging from ₹1 lakh to ₹25 lakh, plus training/incubation etc. For startups in Maharashtra with >51% women ownership, recognized by DPIIT; with priority/reservation for women from backward classes and economically weaker sections. 6:ICDS / Scheme for Adolescent Girls (Sabla) Nutrition, health, hygiene, training programmes for adolescent girls; special interventions in certain districts. Girls in eligible districts; under Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS) framework. 7:PCMC Educational Aid for Girls In Pimpri-Chinchwad, financial aid (~₹1.5 lakh) for girls to pursue higher education abroad. Girls from PCMC area; through certain welfare department schemes.
